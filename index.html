Set in the heart of Georgia, PEACHES is the story of three generations of Calloway women ~ a grandmother who built an empire from dirt, a granddaughter who ran from it, and a twelve-year-old who refuses to let it go.

A heartwarming, deliciously comedic series about fresh starts and family secrets. Told with the warmth of a Southern kitchen ~ sweet and savory, and cast-iron hot.

An orchard unlike any you've seen before. Pits and all.

This generation's Steel Magnolias.

The Series

One year ago, Jesse Calloway gave up Nashville and a promising songwriting career to come home to Georgia after her brother's sudden death. Now she's raising his twelve-year-old daughter, Birdie, barely holding together her family's once-beloved peach orchard…and counting down the days until she can sell the whole thing and never look back.

Until a man walks into a bar.

And everything changes.

The World

Georgia Peach Country

The Orchard

Georgia's peach industry generates hundreds of millions annually and the Calloways were once at the center of it. The orchard is still beautiful but the books are less so. They're one bad season from losing everything ~ and a buyer has come knocking at exactly the right time.

Or, the wrong time. And the wrong buyer.

The Town

An hour outside of Atlanta. A small community built around the Calloway name. A diner that serves peach cobbler. A church where all the kids were baptized. And a bar where a musician and a lawyer will meet one night without knowing they're about to end up on opposite sides of everything.

The Threat

A buyer has arrived with a generous offer, a warm handshake, and a story about saving family farms. But behind the smoke and mirrors is a firm out of Atlanta with an agenda. They're not preserving farms. They're acquiring land, water rights, and far more. They don't care about the Calloway peaches. They care about what's underneath.

The Heart

PEACHES is a love story ~ or two, all tangled up in one. Jesse and Noah have chemistry that starts with a lie and has got nowhere to go but south. She's falling for a man whose last name is on the paperwork that could take everything away; and he's having a real bad day for a whole year.

Meanwhile, Casey Greer, the guy Jesse left without a goodbye, is right next door with ten years of unresolved feelings and a mighty short fuse. For the record, peaches when thrown, hurt.

The Comedy

PEACHES finds its humor in the sweet, mushy bits of life. A woman who can write a song that makes you cry but can't keep a snarky twelve-year-old from outsmarting her before breakfast. A corporate lawyer in $400 shoes learning what a rootstock is. An eighty-year-old matriarch who answers every question about the future with a story about Nixon. A town where every barstool and barbecue knows your damn business.

“Everything looks peachy, until you squeeze.”

ENGINE

Jesse Calloway doesn't want to save the family orchard. She wants to sign the papers, take the money, and disappear back into a life that doesn't remind her of everyone she's lost. But when a one-night encounter with a sharp-tongued lawyer turns into the discovery that the buyer circling her family's land isn't what they seem, she's pulled into a fight she never asked for.

With only her twelve-year-old niece (who may be smarter than all of them), an eighty-year-old grandmother guarding a dangerous secret, and a man from the wrong side of the deal who's risking everything to help her, Jesse has to learn how to farm, how to parent, and how to stay ~ the one thing she's never been able to do.

Each episode deepens a mystery amidst relentless pressure ~ weather that won't cooperate, a harvest window that waits for no one, and a crew that's being quietly poached. Neighboring farmers are selling out, a love triangle simmers, and beneath it all, two secrets ~ one buried in the land, one in the past ~ threaten to surface and change everything the Calloways thought they knew about their family, their orchard, and the brother whose death brought them all back together.

PEACHES is a show about small farms and big lies. About a town worth fighting for and a family learning, one bruising season at a time, that the things most worth keeping are the ones you have to tend every single day.

The Creator

Writer / Director / Producer

He has most recently worked on a Skydance series starring Tim McGraw.

He has created branding campaigns and directed spots for the likes of Ford Motor Company, Liberty Mutual, Lincoln Motor Company, The Ad Council, and Feeding America to name a few.
